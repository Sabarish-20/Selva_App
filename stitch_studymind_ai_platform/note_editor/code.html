The investigation of neural oscillations provides a window into the temporal dynamics of brain communication. Specifically, gamma oscillations (30-80 Hz) have been heavily implicated in the process of feature binding and attention regulation within the visual cortex.

Consider the current models of phase-amplitude coupling. When the phase of a lower-frequency oscillation (like theta) modulates the amplitude of a higher-frequency oscillation, it suggests a hierarchical organization of information processing. This cross-frequency coupling might be the mechanism by which the brain integrates information across different spatial and temporal scales.
